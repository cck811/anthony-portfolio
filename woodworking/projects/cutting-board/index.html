Dual-Purpose Cutting Board

A functional and handcrafted board serving as both a cutting board and serving tray.

Overview

This project explores the intersection of functionality and craftsmanship. The cutting board was designed to be double-sided — one for cutting, the other for serving — featuring contrasting hardwoods, a juice groove, and a custom handle.

Design Process

I began with a mind map to clarify my design goals — a functional board that could serve as both a cutting surface and a serving tray. I wanted a juice groove for practical use, a comfortable handle for serving, and visual interest created through contrasting hardwoods.

After exploring multiple sketch ideas, I finalized a pattern involving maple, cherry, and walnut. I purchased rough lumber from Hughes Hardwood and brought it into the shop to mill all surfaces flat and square. This ensured tight glue joints throughout the lamination process.

I laminated maple with cherry for one strip and maple with walnut for another. Then I crosscut the panels at a 30-degree angle and glued them together to form a chevron-like pattern. Once dry, I cut the piece into strips and laminated them again to amplify the pattern.

Due to the end-grain surface, I built a router flattening jig to level the board instead of using a planer. Afterward, I sanded it flat, cut it to final dimensions, and added a mitered walnut border.

I created a juice groove jig and routed the groove cleanly around the cutting side. On the opposite side, I chiseled out a mortise for a custom metal handle, making the board easier to use as a serving tray. A round-over bit softened the edges for comfort.

After sanding from 80 to 400 grit (including popping the grain with water), I finished the board with food-safe mineral oil and beeswax. I installed the handle and rubber feet to ensure both function and comfort in daily use.

Reflection

This cutting board project deepened my skills in woodworking, especially in milling, pattern lamination, and jig-making. I gained hands-on experience with end-grain construction, finishing techniques, and multi-functional design. The final product balances aesthetics with purpose and reflects my growing attention to detail and usability in design.
