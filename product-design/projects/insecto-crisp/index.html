Insecto Crisp

Sustainable packaging design for mealworm-based tortilla chips

Overview

Insecto Crisp is a packaging design project focused on branding a new line of sustainable mealworm tortilla chips. The goal was to appeal to adventurous eaters, eco-conscious consumers, and health-conscious snackers through bold, clean visuals and cohesive identity. This project combines product positioning, packaging dielines, logo design, and graphic standards.

Design Goals

  • Create immediate brand recognition through visual consistency
  • Highlight the product's sustainability and nutritional value
  • Appeal to adventurous, health-conscious, and eco-aware audiences
  • Develop packaging, display units, and promotional assets

Audience & Strategy

The mood board helped establish the visual and emotional tone for Insecto Crisp. Drawing from modern organic brands and youth-oriented health snacks, the direction embraced bold colors, playful type, and clean geometric composition. The goal was to create a brand that feels both innovative and approachable—inviting eco-conscious millennials and Gen Z consumers to engage with sustainable food alternatives without the discomfort often associated with insect-based products.

Branding & Visual Identity

The logo was developed around the hexagonal shape referencing insect eyes and natural geometry. A stylized mealworm serves as the brand icon. The graphic standard included color palette, typography, business systems (envelope, card, letterhead), and mock applications across digital and physical platforms.

Packaging Design

The dieline was developed to balance shelf presence and practicality. Visual hierarchy ensures the product’s benefits are quickly communicated. The layout uses curved typography and bold product codes to attract attention while remaining clean and minimal. The prototype featured eco-conscious materials and a resealable structure.

Positioning the Brand

With its bold visual identity and emphasis on eco-conscious snacking, Insecto Crisp was designed to provoke conversation and curiosity. From the tone of the packaging copy to the graphic standard system, every touchpoint aims to normalize insect-based protein as a modern, adventurous choice. The product doesn't just occupy a shelf space, it also invites consumers to rethink sustainable food and embrace the future of protein alternatives.

Reflection

Insecto Crisp challenged me to design within the intersection of sustainability, marketing, and visual storytelling. The need to appeal to a broad demographic while promoting an unconventional product pushed me to develop a system that is both accessible and bold. Through ideation, prototyping, and brand system development, I gained insight into how packaging design can shift public perception and promote sustainable practices.
