Magic Toy Box

A playful blend of circuitry, CAD, and sleight of hand

Overview

This whimsical project brings together electronics, 3D printing, and mechanical design to create a magician-inspired interactive toy box. Inspired by a magician illustration on a board game, this box lets users drop in a coin and then perform a little magic trick — swapping the coin via a hidden mechanical disc.

How It Works

Users place a coin into a visible slot on top of a rotating disc. Once placed, they close the lid. On the back of the toy box is a concealed button that activates a servo motor, rotating the disc and swapping out the coin with another underneath — creating the illusion that the coin has magically changed or vanished.

Tools & Materials

  • Adafruit Circuit Playground Express
  • Servo motor for disc rotation
  • Custom-designed 3D printed parts
  • CAD modeling (for housing and mechanical disc)
  • Soldering and assembly tools

Design Process

The initial idea emerged from playful visual inspiration and the desire to experiment with electronics and motion. I designed the disc and coin housing in CAD, allowing for precise fit and function. After printing prototypes, I refined the dimensions and wiring to ensure reliable performance. The shell was designed to hide the mechanism while allowing easy interaction with both the coin slot and hidden button.

Reflections

This small-scale project helped me explore combining play with mechanical interaction. It deepened my understanding of servo motor calibration, component housing design, and circuit integration in tight spaces. More than anything, it reaffirmed my love for playful design and building with both imagination and precision.
