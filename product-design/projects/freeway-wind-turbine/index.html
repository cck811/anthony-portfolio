Freeway Wind Turbine

Harvesting wind energy from freeway traffic

Overview

This short product design study explores a speculative concept to harvest free energy from a commonly wasted source: wind generated by fast-moving vehicles on freeways. The project aims to reframe overlooked byproducts of transportation as opportunities for sustainable innovation.

Initial Concept

The idea began with a simple question: can we reclaim the wind resistance generated when a car moves? Initially, I explored mounting a device directly onto the vehicle, but realized this would only worsen fuel efficiency. I then shifted the concept to focus on capturing wind from the freeway environment itself — without altering the car's performance.

Design Process

Through research into wind-catching forms and turbine geometry, I developed sketches that prioritized low-profile yet efficient energy capture. I built a simple paper prototype to test the proportions and appearance in physical form.

Reflection

Though conceptual, this project challenged me to think critically about sustainability, invisible energy flows, and speculative infrastructure. It emphasized how small design changes—when scaled—could contribute to energy recovery in overlooked spaces.
